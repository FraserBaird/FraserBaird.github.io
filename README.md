# Invitation to Participate in the Ground Level Enhancement in Hydrological Neutron Sensors: Coordination and Analysis Project 
## On behalf of
## Dr Fraser Baird, Prof Trenton Franz, and Prof Keith Ryden

## Project Aims
The Ground Level Enhancement in Hydrological Neutron Sensors: Coordination and Analysis  (GLEHNSCAN) Project has been established in response to the Ground Level Enhancement (GLE) which occurred on November 11th 2025. GLEs are space weather events and natural hazards; during one, ionising radiation levels in the atmosphere increase, occasionally considerably. The resulting radiation field can be a hazard for aviation in particular. 
GLEs are typically very anisotropic and spatially dense observations are necessary to fully understand the event. Because Cosmic Ray Neutron Sensing (CRNS) detectors are sensitive to changes in atmospheric radiation, they provide a novel means of providing these observations with unprecedented spatial resolution. The GLE of November 2025 was the first substantial event since the popularisation of the CRNS technique, and therefore presents a unique opportunity to create an extremely valuable and impactful dataset. The aim of the GLEHNSCAN project are:

  -	to collect as much event data from CRNS operators as possible, and

  -	to analyse and publish the resulting dataset.

We hope to make this dataset as extensive as possible so please do forward this invite on to any CRNS operators you believe may be interested.

## What We Are Asking For
In short, we are asking CRNS operators to provide us with their data during the GLE in exchange for co-authorship of the dataset and accompanying paper. We intend to publish the dataset under a Creative Commons Attribution Share-Alike license. The specifics of the data we are requesting are below:

### Time period:

2025-11-11 00:00 to 2025-11-12 12:00 (UTC)

### Primary Data
-	Raw neutron counts
-	Atmospheric Pressure
-	Absolute Humidity or Atmospheric Temperature and Relative Humidity (external to the sensor)
### Secondary Data (Optional)
-	Independent local soil moisture measurements
-	Rainfall observations
### Metadata (required)
-	Site location (latitude, longitude, altitude)
-	Sensor manufacturer and model
  	Any modifications to the sensor

## What We Can Offer
In exchange for data-sharing, we will list all authors of an operator’s data as co-authors of the overall dataset and accompanying paper. In addition, we will host a virtual workshop near the end of 2026 focussing on the impact of space weather on CRNS signals for project participants.
